Two different realities of the divided Korea

BY LISA KIM

Published Aug. 8, 2021

NEW YORK -- This July marked the 68th anniversary of the end of the Korean War. (The Korean War happened between 1950 and 1953.) Koreans in the two Koreas have since lived completely different lives though it is difficult to grasp the status quo of the reclusive state of North Korea.

Figures provided by Statistics Korea give outsiders a glimpse into North Korea. Women born in the North, on average, lived around 12 years shorter than their Southern counterparts. The life expectancy of North Korean women was 74 years old compared with South Korean women's figure of 86, according to Statistics Korea.

In terms of wealth, North Korea's GDP per capita stood at 1.4 million won, which is equivalent to about 1,200 US dollars, in 2019, data showed. That was around a quarter of South Korea's number.
